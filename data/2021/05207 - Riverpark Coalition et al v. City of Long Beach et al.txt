2 1. Over extensive public opposition and in violation of the California Environmental

3 Quality Act (*CEQA") and the Long Beach Municipal Code, on April 13, 2021, the City of

4 Long Beach approved the Pacific Place Project (*Project") based on a mitigated negative

5 declaration ("MND") instead of an environmental impact report ("EIR"), even though a fair

6 argument exists that the Project will have significant adverse environmental impacts.

7 2. The Project would replace over 19 acres of open space along the Los Angeles

8 River with a large-scale commercial warehouse, storage, office, and parking lot development

0 that would have adverse impacts on the environment with regard to air quality, aesthetics,

10 biological resources, geology/soils, hazards/hazardous materials, historic and cultural resources,

11 tribal resources, and hydrology and water quality, among other impacts. The Project will also

12 significantly reduce any possible relief and recreational opportunities in the area, whereas

13 currently the surrounding community would be able to use portions of this space and nearby

14 areas for passive and active recreation.

15 3. The City's MND lacks substantial evidence to support many of its conclusions that
16 the Project's potential adverse impacts on the environment will not be significant.

17 4. Since a fair argument exists that the Project will have significant adverse impacts

18 on historic resources, aesthetics, biological resources, hydrology and water quality, and

19 recreation, and since the MND fails entirely to acknowledge, analyze, or fully mitigate these
potential impacts to a level below significance, the City's approval violates the California 20

21 Environmental Quality Act, and the Project approvals must be rescinded.

12 The Fair Argument Standard Requires Preparation of an EIR

13 43. CEQA prohibits a lead agency from approving a project that may have:

14 significant impact on the environment without first preparing, considering, and certifying an

15 EIR. The City violated CEQA by adopting an MND for the Project, despite substantial

16 evidence supporting a fair argument that the project may have a significant impact on the

17 environment.

18 44. The purpose of the initial study is to provide the lead agency with adequate

19 information regarding project to determine the appropriate environmental review document

20 and "documentation of the factual basis for the finding in a negative declaration that a project

21 will not have a significant effect on the environment." (Ctr. for Sierra Nevada Conservation v.

22 County of El Dorado (2012) 202 Cal. App. 4th 1156, 1170, citations omitted.) There must be

23 basis within the record to support the conclusions reached by the initial study. (Lighthouse

24 Field Beach Rescue v. City of Santa Cruz (2005) 131 Cal.App.4th 1170, 1201.) "Where an

25 agency. . . fails to gather information and undertake an adequate environmental analysis in its

26 initial study, a negative declaration is inappropriate." (El Dorado County Taxpayers for

27 Quality Growth v. County of El Dorado (2004) 122 Cal. App. 4th 1591, 1597, citations

28 omitted.)
